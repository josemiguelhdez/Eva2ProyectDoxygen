Descripción detallada

The correlated vector mutations stores a "velocity" vector for each individual, updates the velocity by rotation and scaling, and then mutates the individual by adding the velocity. This was used for a particle filter localization problem and is less useful in general.

Rotation vectors are normal distributed with mean zero, scaling factors are log-normally distributed around mean 1. This means that the averaged expected change of the mutation vector is zero. The smaller the deviations, the higher the correlations between successive mutation steps.
